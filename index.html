Remote Working Manifesto

We are uncovering better ways to organize the way we work remotely.
In our journey, we consider the following key assumptions.

Ownership, freedom, trust, and transparency are the foundations of our remote working.

We believe that people and their interactions are more important than processes and tools.

We talk about the results of work, rather than the time spent.

Teamwork, pair programming, and human relations are essential, but we prefer asynchronous to synchronous communication.

We love automating and documenting repetitive tasks, rather than performing the same manual actions over and over again.

We prefer flexible working hours over set working hours.

We believe it is fundamental to define the boundaries of space and time between work and personal life.

We think it is not important where you work, but that your environment shapes the way your work.
